Discover Wharepuke Falls

Nestled in the lush native bush just minutes from Kerikeri town center, Wharepuke Falls is one of the area's most charming yet lesser-known waterfalls. This hidden gem offers visitors a more secluded experience away from the crowds that typically gather at the more famous Rainbow Falls.

The name "Wharepuke" comes from the Māori language, where "whare" means house and "puke" refers to a hill or mound, indicating the falls' location on elevated terrain. The waterfall cascades gracefully over ancient volcanic rocks, creating a series of gentle steps rather than a single dramatic drop.

What makes Wharepuke Falls special is its intimate setting, surrounded by native ferns, nikau palms, and other indigenous flora. The tranquil pool at the base of the falls is perfect for a refreshing dip on a hot summer day, with the water being slightly warmer than other swimming spots in the area.

Located within the Wharepuke Subtropical Garden, visitors can combine their waterfall experience with a stroll through one of New Zealand's most diverse plant collections, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers and photographers.

Quick Facts

Height:
Approximately 5 meters (16 feet)
Type:
Cascading stepped waterfall
Water Source:
Wharepuke Stream
Swimming:
Good for swimming, family-friendly
Walking Time:
15 minutes from garden entrance
Difficulty:
Easy to moderate - Some uneven terrain
Best Time:
After rainfall for better flow, morning for photography

Visitor Tips

  • Visit early morning for the best lighting and fewer visitors.
  • Wear sturdy footwear as the track can be slippery after rain.
  • Bring insect repellent during summer months.
  • The falls are most impressive after rainfall.
  • Allow at least 30-45 minutes to fully enjoy the falls and surroundings.
  • Respect the natural environment by staying on marked paths.
  • Photography is best in the morning when light filters through the trees.

Access Information

Wharepuke Falls is easily accessible:

Location:
Within Wharepuke Subtropical Garden, Kerikeri
Parking:
Available at the garden entrance
Track:
Well-maintained but can be slippery when wet
Facilities:
Café and toilets at garden entrance

Natural Environment

Wharepuke Falls is set within one of Kerikeri's most diverse ecological areas. The surrounding subtropical garden hosts an impressive collection of rare and exotic plants from around the world, creating a unique backdrop for this natural waterfall.

The native bush around the falls features nikau palms, tree ferns, and other indigenous species that create a lush, verdant environment. This rich ecosystem supports various native birds, making it a great spot for birdwatching.

The clear waters of the Wharepuke Stream are home to native fish species, and the pristine quality of the water is testament to the careful environmental management of the area.

Conservation efforts focus on maintaining the natural character of the falls while allowing sustainable visitor access, ensuring this special place remains protected for future generations.
